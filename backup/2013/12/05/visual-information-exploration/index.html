Lectures & Workshops

Visual Information Exploration

December 5, 2013 Sebastian Meier

In winter 2013/14 i was collaborating with Marian Dörk on a lecture at the interface design department at the Potsdam university of applied sciences. In the course titled “visual information exploration” we were looking into basics of information visualization as well as search and exploratory interfaces.

Student-Projects

  • SoundSky

    The prototype allowed users to explore their music network on soundcloud and discover relationships and new music.

    Project by Henrik Waffner

  • Vistory

    Google Chrome extension that visualizes a user's browsing history allowing her to find patterns over time.

    Project by Gabriel Credico

  • Culturegraphy

    As a part of his master thesis Kim explored  relationships of movies stored in the IMDB.

    Project by Kim Albrech | Projectwebsite

  • Food additives

    A visualization and search interface for food additives, taking modern data based storytelling into account.

    Project by Andreas Schüßler

  • Lifelines

    The project explores ways of visualizing a persons geographic biography to use it as a visual storytelling tool

    Project by Chris Trost

  • My World 2015

    The project explores the usage of scented widgets to explore the data gathered by the "My World 2015" survey by the UN.

    Project by Martin von Lupin

  • OPAC

    The project takes a new visual approach on interfaces for the visual exploration of library catalogues. 

    Project by Christoph Wiedenmann
